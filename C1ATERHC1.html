John Holbrook 08/22/2016 05:53 PM
Things I dislike about the electoral college: 1. The person-to-elector ratio is not consistent state-to-state. Because each state gets a number of electors equal to the sum of their senators and representatives, and senators are not distributed based on population, states with small populations have more electors per capita than large states. Wyoming gets one elector for every 195000 people, whereas CA gets one elector per 711000 people. So arguably one vote in Wyoming is worth 3.5 votes in California. Because of this, it’s mathematically possible (though obviously unlikely) to win the presidency with under a third of the popular vote, which is stupid. 2. Related problem: because states award electors on a winner-take-all basis, people in uncompetitive states who voted for the loser essentially get their votes thrown out. Specifically, there’s no way in hell Hillary’s gonna win WV, so there’s little incentive for her supporters in WV to vote for her. If the president were elected by national popular vote, then democrats in predominantly red states and republicans in predominantly blue states would have a greater incentive to cast their ballots. Further viewing/reading: http://www.cgpgrey.com/blog/the-trouble-with-the-electoral-college.html
